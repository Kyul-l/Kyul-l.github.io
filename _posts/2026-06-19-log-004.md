The city stopped breathing around 1 a.m. I was on the balcony with the light off, watching a plane drift so slow it didn't feel like a plane — more like a punctuation mark inching across a page.

Air with no wind in it. The kind of stillness that doesn't feel calm, just paused. As if the whole night were waiting for something to happen and had already made up its mind not to explain what.

I stayed there long enough that the cold stopped registering. Long enough that I forgot which floor I was on. Long enough to feel, for the first time in a week, that nothing needed to be resolved.

Then a bird made a single sound somewhere below — one note, unremarkable — and everything started again.
